# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## Scrum Master

### Role Summary
The Scrum Master facilitates Scrum ceremonies, removes impediments, and coaches the team on Agile practices. They protect the team from external distractions and scope creep, and work closely with Project Managers and Developers to sustain delivery pace and drive continuous improvement.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate daily standups, sprint planning, sprint reviews, and retrospectives
- Identify and remove blockers that impede team progress
- Coach the team and organization on Scrum and Agile principles
- Shield the team from unplanned interruptions and scope creep
- Track sprint metrics (velocity, burn-down) and surface trends to stakeholders

### Goals
- Maintain a healthy, self-organizing team
- Improve team velocity and predictability over time
- Foster a culture of continuous improvement

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint ceremonies
- Retrospective action items and improvement tracking
- Impediment logs and escalation paths when needed

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure that products are user-centered, translating requirements into wireframes, prototypes, and design specifications. They act as a feedback loop between stakeholders and the delivery team, collaborating closely with Product Managers and Developers.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing to inform design decisions
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity design assets
- Translate business and product requirements into intuitive user experiences
- Collaborate with Developers during implementation to maintain design fidelity
- Gather and incorporate feedback from stakeholders and end users

### Goals
- Deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences
- Reduce rework caused by late-stage usability issues
- Ensure consistency across product interfaces

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews and critique sessions
- Annotated wireframes and prototype links shared with engineering
- Stakeholder demos of UX flows during sprint reviews

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
The QA Lead owns the overall test strategy and quality metrics for a project. They coordinate manual and automated testing efforts, collaborate with Developers and Project Managers to prioritize bug fixes, and ensure the definition of done includes adequate test coverage and acceptance criteria.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain the test strategy, including scope of manual and automated testing
- Review acceptance criteria and ensure testability before development begins
- Coordinate test execution across the team and track defect resolution
- Maintain quality metrics (defect density, test coverage, escape rate) and report to stakeholders
- Collaborate with Developers to establish and enforce the definition of done

### Goals
- Prevent defects from reaching production
- Continuously improve test automation coverage and reliability
- Provide clear quality signal to the team before each release

### Typical Communication
- Test plans and test case repositories shared with the team
- Bug triage meetings with Developers and Project Managers
- Quality status reports included in sprint reviews

---

## DevOps Specialist

### Role Summary
The DevOps Specialist manages CI/CD pipelines, deployment automation, and production monitoring. They work with Developers to ensure reliable, repeatable deployments and lead incident response and performance management activities.

### Responsibilities
- Design, build, and maintain CI/CD pipelines and deployment infrastructure
- Automate build, test, and release processes to reduce manual toil
- Monitor production systems and respond to incidents with appropriate urgency
- Collaborate with Developers on infrastructure-as-code and environment configuration
- Define and track reliability metrics (uptime, MTTR, deployment frequency)

### Goals
- Enable fast, safe, and frequent software delivery
- Minimize production incidents and mean time to recovery
- Increase infrastructure reliability and observability

### Typical Communication
- Runbooks and incident post-mortems shared with Engineering and Project Managers
- Pipeline and deployment status updates during sprint reviews
- On-call schedules and alerting documentation

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts elicit and document requirements, manage stakeholder expectations, and clarify goals. They bridge Product Managers and Developers, ensuring business needs are accurately translated into well-defined technical outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Facilitate requirements-gathering workshops with stakeholders and subject-matter experts
- Document functional and non-functional requirements, use cases, and process flows
- Validate that proposed solutions align with business objectives
- Manage and track requirements changes throughout the project lifecycle
- Support acceptance testing by clarifying requirements and expected behaviors

### Goals
- Ensure requirements are complete, unambiguous, and agreed upon before development begins
- Reduce rework caused by misunderstood or missing requirements
- Maintain a clear and traceable link between business goals and delivered features

### Typical Communication
- Requirements documents and user-story refinement sessions
- Stakeholder sign-off meetings and change-request logs
- Collaboration with Developers during sprint planning to clarify acceptance criteria
